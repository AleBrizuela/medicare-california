Medicare in San Diego County

San Diego is one of California's fastest-growing senior communities, with over 550,000 Medicare-eligible residents spread across one of the most geographically diverse counties in the state. From coastal neighborhoods in San Diego proper to sprawling suburbs in Chula Vista and Escondido, and rural areas in East County, each region has distinct healthcare options. This geographic diversity means your ZIP code (whether 92101 in downtown, 92103 in City Heights, or 92110 in Ocean Beach) significantly impacts your available plans and healthcare networks.
Kaiser Permanente and SCAN Health Plan are the dominant carriers in San Diego, each with extensive networks and strong reputations. SCAN particularly dominates the San Diego market with competitive Medicare Advantage rates and excellent local relationships. Alignment Healthcare has made significant inroads in the San Diego market, appealing to cost-conscious seniors. Blue Shield of California offers solid coverage throughout the county with good provider networks. For those preferring Traditional Medicare, local Medigap supplemental plans are widely available. Scripps Health, UC San Diego Health, and Sharp Healthcare are the major medical systems, and most Medicare plans include these in-network, though you should always verify your specific doctor and facility.
San Diego's climate and lifestyle attract many retirees, and the county's plan selection reflects that demand. Residents in ZIP codes like 92101, 92103, and 92110 benefit from competitive plan offerings. The county also has significant Medicare-eligible populations in suburbs like Chula Vista (91910 area), Escondido (92025 area), and Oceanside (92054 area), each with strong local plan availability. Given San Diego's sprawl and diverse healthcare infrastructure, choosing the right Medicare plan requires understanding your specific location and preferred healthcare facilities—something our local agents excel at.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many Medicare plans are available in San Diego?

San Diego County typically has 40+ Medicare Advantage plans available, depending on your specific ZIP code. Additionally, you can choose Traditional Medicare (Parts A & B) with Medigap supplemental insurance and standalone Part D plans. The extensive selection means you have genuine flexibility in finding a plan that fits your healthcare needs, budget, and preferred doctors. Our agents can help you evaluate all options specific to your neighborhood.

Can I keep my UC San Diego Health or Scripps doctor with Medicare?

Most Medicare plans in San Diego include UC San Diego Health and Scripps Healthcare in their networks, but this varies by specific plan. Kaiser Permanente has its own network and may not include these systems. Always verify your preferred doctor and hospital are in-network before enrolling in any plan. Our agents can check provider networks for you—it's one of the most important decisions when choosing Medicare coverage in San Diego.

When is the best time to enroll in Medicare in San Diego?

You're typically eligible at age 65 and have a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period to sign up without penalties. Many San Diego residents use the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15-December 7) to review and switch plans. If you're retiring or losing employer coverage, you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period. Even if you've already delayed enrollment, our agents can help explain your options and any potential penalties—it's never too late to get expert guidance.
