Medicare in Los Angeles County

Los Angeles County is one of the largest Medicare markets in the nation, with more beneficiaries than most states. This means you have exceptional choice when selecting your Medicare plan. The county spans diverse neighborhoods from coastal areas in Santa Monica and Long Beach to the San Fernando Valley and inland communities in Pasadena and San Gabriel. Each area has different healthcare systems and plan availability, which is why localized guidance matters.
Kaiser Permanente dominates the LA market with excellent in-network coverage throughout the county. However, many residents also benefit from SCAN Health Plan, which offers competitive rates and strong coverage in LA and Orange County. Alignment Healthcare has grown significantly in the LA market, appealing to those seeking lower premiums. Blue Shield of California provides broad coverage options, and Traditional Medicare combined with Medigap or Part D plans remains a solid choice for many residents. The Los Angeles County+USC Medical Center, Cedars-Sinai Medical Center, and UCLA Health are major healthcare systems that most plans either include in-network or allow you to access with reasonable out-of-pocket costs.
For residents in ZIP codes like 90001 (Downtown LA), 90012 (Downtown LA), and 90028 (Los Feliz), plan options are abundant. Coastal communities in 90210 (Beverly Hills) and 90401 (Santa Monica) often see different plan concentrations. The complexity of LA's Medicare landscape—combined with the diversity of neighborhoods, healthcare facilities, and language preferences—makes working with knowledgeable local agents invaluable for making the right decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many Medicare plans are available in Los Angeles?

Los Angeles County typically has 50+ Medicare Advantage plans available, depending on your specific ZIP code. Additionally, you can choose Traditional Medicare (Parts A & B) combined with Medigap supplemental insurance and standalone Part D prescription drug plans. This level of choice gives LA residents flexibility in finding a plan that matches their healthcare needs and budget. Our agents can help you navigate all options specific to your neighborhood.

Can I keep my current doctor at Cedars-Sinai or UCLA Health with Medicare?

Whether you can keep your current doctor depends on which plan you choose. Kaiser Permanente has strong affiliated relationships throughout LA and may restrict you to their network. Other plans like Blue Shield and SCAN typically include major LA health systems in their networks. If keeping a specific doctor is important, we recommend verifying plan networks before enrolling. Our agents can check if your doctor is in-network for any plan you're considering.

When is the best time to enroll in Medicare in Los Angeles?

Most people become eligible at 65 and have a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period. However, many LA residents delay enrollment and can use the General Enrollment Period (January 1-March 31) to sign up. If you already have Medicare, the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15-December 7) is the best time to review and switch plans. Special circumstances like losing job coverage may qualify you for a Special Enrollment Period. It's never too late to get guidance—our agents can explain your options regardless of your timeline.
