Medicare Open Enrollment Ends March 31 in California: What to Do Now

Published March 24, 2026 • Susana Marcos LLC, Licensed Medicare Insurance Agent • California

The clock is ticking. The Medicare Open Enrollment Period (OEP) closes on March 31, 2026, and California beneficiaries have just one week left to make plan changes. If your current Medicare Advantage plan is not working for you, this is your last chance until fall to switch.

This article walks you through what you can still do before the deadline, who this window applies to, and how to make a smart, last-minute decision without regret.

What Is the Medicare Open Enrollment Period?

The OEP runs every year from January 1 through March 31. It is different from the Annual Enrollment Period (AEP), which happens in the fall. During the OEP, you can:

  • Switch from one Medicare Advantage plan to a different Medicare Advantage plan
  • Disenroll from Medicare Advantage and return to Original Medicare (Parts A and B)
  • If you return to Original Medicare, enroll in a standalone Part D prescription drug plan
Important: The OEP does NOT allow you to switch from Original Medicare into a Medicare Advantage plan. That can only happen during the AEP (October 15 through December 7) or at your Initial Enrollment Period.

Why California Beneficiaries Should Pay Attention Right Now

California has one of the most competitive Medicare Advantage markets in the country. Dozens of plans operate across Los Angeles, San Diego, the Bay Area, and inland communities. That variety is great, but it also means it is easy to be in the wrong plan without knowing it.

Common reasons Californians switch during OEP

Since January 1, many beneficiaries have discovered that their 2026 plan made changes they did not expect. Network narrowing is a frequent issue, especially for people in the Inland Empire and Central Valley who depend on specific specialists. Drug formulary changes are another common surprise: a medication covered last year may now require a higher tier copay or prior authorization.

Others simply enrolled in a plan during AEP based on the premium and realized the out-of-pocket costs or provider restrictions do not fit their real-life situation.

How to Make a Last-Minute Plan Switch Before March 31

Step 1: Review your current plan's pain points

Make a short list of what is not working. Is it your network? Your drug costs? Dental or vision benefits you expected but are not using? Knowing your specific frustration will help you find a better-fit plan quickly.

Step 2: Use Medicare Plan Finder

Go to Medicare.gov/plan-compare and enter your zip code and medications. California-specific plans will appear with side-by-side cost comparisons. Pay close attention to the maximum out-of-pocket limit, which caps your annual exposure in Original Medicare-covered services.

Step 3: Verify your doctors are in-network

Before you switch, call each new plan's member services line or check its online directory to confirm your primary care physician and any specialists you see regularly are in the plan's network. Do not assume; networks change every year.

Step 4: Call a local licensed agent

What Happens After March 31?

Once the OEP closes, you are generally locked into your current plan until December 31, 2026, unless you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period (SEP). SEPs are triggered by specific life events such as:

  • Moving out of your plan's service area
  • Losing employer-sponsored coverage
  • Qualifying for both Medicare and Medicaid (Medi-Cal in California)
  • Your plan losing its Medicare contract or leaving your area

If none of those apply to you, the next open window to change plans is the AEP, which begins October 15. For tips on how to prepare for that, see our guide on Medicare Annual Enrollment Period in California.

Turning 65 Soon? Your Situation Is Different

If you are turning 65 in the coming months, the OEP does not apply to you in the same way. You have your own Initial Enrollment Period (IEP), a 7-month window that begins 3 months before your birthday month. Missing your IEP can result in Part B and Part D late enrollment penalties that follow you for life.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the Medicare Open Enrollment Period end in 2026?

March 31, 2026. Changes you make by that date take effect April 1.

Can I switch from Medicare Advantage back to Original Medicare during OEP?

Yes. You can drop your Medicare Advantage plan and return to Original Medicare, then add a standalone Part D drug plan. Keep in mind that in California, Medigap (Medicare Supplement) insurers can apply underwriting outside of guaranteed-issue periods, so timing matters if you want supplemental coverage too.

What happens if I miss the Medicare OEP deadline in California?

You will stay in your current plan through the end of 2026, unless a qualifying life event creates a Special Enrollment Period for you.

Is there a penalty for not enrolling in Medicare on time in California?

Yes. Late enrollment in Part B adds a 10% penalty for every 12-month period you were eligible but not enrolled. Part D penalties are also permanent and calculated monthly.
