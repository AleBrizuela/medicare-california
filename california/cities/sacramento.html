Medicare in Sacramento County

Sacramento County is a growing Medicare market with approximately 280,000 beneficiaries spread across California's state capital and surrounding communities. As a mid-sized market, Sacramento offers good plan selection and competition without the overwhelming complexity of larger urban markets. ZIP codes like 95814, 95825, and 95835 serve different neighborhoods, from downtown Sacramento to suburban areas, and each has distinct healthcare facility access and plan availability.
Kaiser Permanente has a strong presence in Sacramento with integrated Northern California operations and good local medical facilities. Blue Shield of California offers broad network coverage throughout the county with connections to major healthcare facilities. UnitedHealth Group plans provide competitive options, and Aetna also participates in the market. UC Davis Health is the dominant academic medical system serving Sacramento and surrounding regions, and most Medicare plans include it or offer reasonable access. Sutter Health operates multiple facilities throughout the county. For traditional Medicare seekers, Medigap supplemental plans are readily available throughout Sacramento County.
Sacramento's Medicare landscape offers a good balance—more plan options than smaller regional markets, but simpler to navigate than mega-markets like LA or San Diego. Whether you're in the walkable neighborhoods of downtown (95814) or more car-dependent suburban areas (95835), you'll have solid healthcare options. Government workers comprise a significant portion of Sacramento's senior population, which influences plan choices and preferred health systems. Our local agents understand these unique Sacramento preferences and can guide you to Medicare coverage that aligns with your healthcare needs and retirement lifestyle.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many Medicare plans are available in Sacramento?

Sacramento County typically has 30+ Medicare Advantage plans available, depending on your specific ZIP code. Beyond Medicare Advantage, you can also choose Traditional Medicare (Parts A & B) combined with Medigap supplemental insurance and standalone Part D prescription drug plans. This solid selection gives Sacramento residents genuine flexibility in finding plans that match their healthcare needs and budget. Our agents can help you understand all options available in your specific area.

Can I keep my UC Davis Health doctor with Medicare?

Most Medicare plans in Sacramento include UC Davis Health in their networks, though specific provider coverage varies by plan. Kaiser Permanente has its own network and may restrict access to independent UC Davis providers. If keeping a specific doctor at UC Davis Health is important, verify they're in-network before enrolling. Our agents can check whether your doctor participates in any plan you're considering, ensuring continuity of care.

When is the best time to enroll in Medicare in Sacramento?

Most people become eligible at 65 with a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period. Many Sacramento residents use the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15-December 7) to review and switch plans. If you're transitioning from a government job or losing employer coverage, you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period. Our agents can explain your options and help you understand the timeline that's best for your situation.
