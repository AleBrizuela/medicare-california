What is Dual Eligibility?

Dual eligibility is a special enrollment status where you qualify for both Medicare and Medi-Cal (California's Medicaid program) at the same time. This dual coverage combination is one of the most valuable healthcare benefits available to California seniors with limited income.

Here is how it works fundamentally:

  • Medicare is your primary insurance. It covers hospital stays, doctor visits, tests, preventive care, and prescription drugs.
  • Medi-Cal is your secondary insurance. It covers the costs that Medicare does not, including deductibles, copayments, coinsurance, dental, vision, hearing, and long-term care.

The result: You pay significantly less out-of-pocket for healthcare, often zero dollars for many services.

Who Qualifies for Dual Eligibility in California

To qualify for dual eligibility, you must meet specific requirements in four categories. All must be met simultaneously.

Age or Status

  • Age 65 or older, OR
  • Person with a disability, OR
  • End-stage renal disease

Medicare Status

  • Enrolled in Medicare Part A (hospital insurance)
  • Enrolled in Medicare Part B (medical insurance)
  • OR eligible even if not enrolled

Financial Requirements

  • Income below California limits
  • Limited assets (under $2,000 single)
  • Assets verified and documented

Residency and Citizenship

  • California resident
  • U.S. citizen or qualified immigrant
  • Proof of identity required

2026 Income and Asset Limits for California

The most common reason people don't qualify for Medi-Cal is exceeding income limits. California's limits are based on 200 percent of the federal poverty level and are updated annually.

Asset Limits (2026)

Maximum non-excluded resources:

  • Single person: $2,000
  • Married couple: $3,000

Resources that DO NOT count toward the limit: Your primary residence, one vehicle, furniture, personal items, prepaid funeral plans, certain savings accounts, life insurance with low face value, and any income-producing property (with specific rules).

What Counts as Income

Gross monthly income calculation includes:

  • Wages, salaries, self-employment income
  • Social Security benefits (full amount)
  • Pension and retirement distributions
  • Rental and property income
  • Interest, dividends, and investment returns
  • Unemployment and disability benefits
  • Alimony and child support received

Income that does NOT count: Supplemental Security Income (SSI), veteran benefits, LIHEAP (utility assistance), certain tribal income, and some state/local benefits.

How Medicare-Medi-Cal Coordination Works

Understanding how the two programs work together is key to maximizing your benefits. The coordination system is specifically designed to eliminate gaps in coverage.

The Primary-Secondary Insurance Model

Medicare is primary: When you receive healthcare, Medicare is billed first. Medicare determines its covered amount based on its fee schedule.

Medi-Cal is secondary: Medi-Cal then covers what Medicare leaves unpaid, including:

  • Your Medicare Part A deductible (hospital insurance deductible)
  • Your Medicare Part B coinsurance (typically 20 percent)
  • Your Medicare Part D cost-sharing for drugs
  • Services not covered by Medicare

Real-World Example: A Doctor Visit

Step by Step: Without vs. With Medi-Cal

  1. You schedule a doctor visit: Your doctor accepts Medicare
  2. Medicare processes the claim: Medicare approves $100 as the allowable fee and pays 80 percent ($80)
  3. Without Medi-Cal: You would owe the remaining 20 percent ($20) as patient responsibility
  4. With Medi-Cal: Medi-Cal sees your responsibility and pays the $20
  5. Your cost: $0 | You pay nothing because Medi-Cal covered what Medicare didn't

Services Medicare Covers

Medicare Part A covers: inpatient hospital care, skilled nursing facility care, hospice care, and home health services. Medicare Part B covers: doctor office visits, outpatient care, preventive services, medical equipment, and some therapies. Medicare Part D covers: prescription medications.

Services Medi-Cal Adds to Medicare

Medi-Cal provides critical coverage that Medicare does not include:

Main Benefits of Dual Eligibility

Dual eligibility provides transformative healthcare benefits for qualifying seniors. These benefits can save thousands of dollars annually and provide peace of mind.

1. Elimination of Copayments and Coinsurance

The most immediate benefit: You typically pay nothing or near-nothing for covered services. Medi-Cal covers Medicare's cost-sharing, which means your doctor visits, hospital stays, and tests have minimal out-of-pocket costs.

2. Full Prescription Drug Coverage

Medicare Part D has coverage limits including deductibles, copayments, and the infamous "donut hole" (coverage gap). Medi-Cal eliminates these barriers by covering Part D costs and extending coverage to medications Medicare rejects.

3. Long-Term Care Protection

Medicare covers only 100 days of skilled nursing facility care after a hospital stay. After those 100 days, the costs are entirely on you unless you have Medi-Cal. Medi-Cal provides unlimited long-term care coverage, protecting your assets from catastrophic nursing home expenses.

4. Comprehensive Dental, Vision, and Hearing Coverage

These services are completely absent from Medicare coverage but fully covered by Medi-Cal. This means free dental cleanings and extractions, complete vision exams and glasses, and hearing aids.

5. Enhanced Preventive Care

Medi-Cal covers additional preventive services beyond Medicare, including behavioral health screening, substance use disorder treatment, and community health worker services.

6. Protection Against Medical Bankruptcy

Without Medi-Cal, a serious health event can cost $10,000-$100,000+. With dual eligibility, your exposure is minimal. This protection preserves your estate for your heirs.

7. Better Access to Specialists

With Medi-Cal handling cost-sharing, specialists become truly affordable. You can seek specialty care without financial barriers.

D-SNP Plans: The Optimal Choice for Dual-Eligible Individuals

D-SNP (Dual Special Needs Plan) plans are specifically designed for dual-eligible people. They integrate Medicare and Medi-Cal into one coordinated plan, offering superior benefits and ease of use compared to managing Medicare and Medi-Cal separately.

What Makes D-SNP Plans Different

A D-SNP is a type of Medicare Advantage plan that combines:

  • Medicare Part A coverage (hospital insurance)
  • Medicare Part B coverage (medical insurance)
  • Medicare Part D coverage (prescription drugs)
  • Integrated Medi-Cal coverage
  • Special additional benefits beyond Medicare

Key difference: Instead of dealing with two separate insurance programs, one plan handles everything. This simplifies billing, eliminates confusion, and ensures coordinated care.

D-SNP Advantages You Get

Zero or minimal copayments: Many plans offer $0 copayments for primary care, specialists, and emergency visits.

Integrated care management: Plans assign a care manager who coordinates your entire healthcare picture across both Medicare and Medi-Cal benefits.

Special additional benefits: Most D-SNP plans include benefits not available elsewhere: transportation to medical appointments (sometimes including non-medical trips), meal delivery programs, fitness benefits, nutrition counseling, and social support services.

Pharmaceutical support: D-SNPs often provide 24/7 pharmacist support, medication therapy management, and integration of Medi-Cal pharmacy coverage.

Provider coordination: Contracted providers are trained in managing dual-eligible populations and coordinating between Medicare and Medi-Cal.

Enrollment Periods for D-SNP Plans

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP): When you first become eligible for Medicare, you have 7 months to enroll (3 months before turning 65, the month of your birthday, and 3 months after).

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP): October 15 through December 7 each year. During this time, you can switch plans or enroll in a new plan.

Special Enrollment Periods (SEP): You may qualify for a SEP if you have a qualifying life event such as moving, losing coverage, or becoming eligible for both Medicare and Medi-Cal.

Medicare Savings Programs (MSP): Additional Help Programs

If your income exceeds Medi-Cal limits but remains low, you may qualify for a Medicare Savings Program (MSP). These programs help pay Medicare premiums and cost-sharing if you don't qualify for full Medi-Cal.

The Four MSP Programs

QMB Program

  • Name: Qualified Medicare Beneficiary
  • Income limit: 100 percent FPL (~$900/month)
  • Coverage: Parts A, B premiums; deductibles; copays; coinsurance

SLMB Program

  • Name: Specified Low-Income Medicare Beneficiary
  • Income limit: 120 percent FPL (~$1,080/month)
  • Coverage: Part B premium only

QI Program

  • Name: Qualifying Individual
  • Income limit: 135 percent FPL (~$1,215/month)
  • Coverage: Part B premium (limited funding)

QDWI Program

  • Name: Qualified Disabled Working Individual
  • For: Working individuals with disabilities who lost Medicare
  • Coverage: Part A premium

Extra Help / Low Income Subsidy (LIS)

Extra Help is a federal program for people with limited income and resources who struggle to pay Medicare Part D (prescription drug) costs.

Income limits: Up to 150 percent of federal poverty level (approximately $1,350/month for a single person).

Savings: Up to 75 percent reduction in Part D drug costs.

Benefits include: Zero or reduced premiums for Part D coverage, minimal or zero deductibles, reduced copayments for medications (often $0-3 per drug).

Applying for Extra Help can be done when you enroll in Part D or any time during the year. You can apply through Social Security (1-800-772-1213) or Medicare.

How to Apply for Medi-Cal in California

Applying for Medi-Cal is free, straightforward, and can be done in multiple ways. Most applications are processed within 30-45 days.

Three Ways to Apply

Online (Fastest)

  • Visit benefitscal.org or dhcs.ca.gov
  • Create account (email required)
  • Enter information
  • Submit and track online
  • Available 24/7

By Phone

  • Call 1-866-352-7222
  • Phone lines open daily
  • Bilingual support available
  • Longest hold times during peak hours
  • Slow for complex situations

In Person: Visit your county Medi-Cal office with all documents. Find your office at dhcs.ca.gov. Fastest for complex applications; bring original documents.

Documents You Will Need

Have these ready to speed up your application. You can apply even if you don't have all documents, but having them makes approval faster.

Complete Document Checklist for Medi-Cal

  1. Proof of identity: Valid driver license, passport, state ID card, or tribal ID
  2. Social Security documentation: Social Security card or number, work authorization document (I-94, visa, employment permit)
  3. Citizenship/legal status: Birth certificate, U.S. passport, I-551 (green card), I-131 (travel document), or naturalization certificate
  4. Residency proof: Utility bill, lease agreement, bank statement, eviction notice, or letter from homeless shelter (dated within 60 days)
  5. Income documentation (last 30 days): Recent pay stubs, tax returns (last 2 years), Social Security award letter, pension statement, rental income receipts, bank statements showing deposits
  6. Asset documentation: Bank statements, investment account statements, property deed or tax bill, vehicle title/registration, life insurance face value
  7. Medicare information: Copy of current Medicare card, Medicare approval notice, or eligibility notice
  8. Family member information: Same documentation for spouse and any dependents you claim
  9. Job information (if applicable): Letter from employer, employment contract, or business tax documents

The Application and Decision Timeline

Submission: Online submissions are processed automatically; phone and in-person applications are input by staff.

Verification (10-20 days): Medi-Cal staff may contact you by phone or mail to verify information, especially income and citizenship.

Decision (45 days max): You receive a notice letter by mail (sometimes by email) informing you of approval or denial. Urgent cases may be expedited to 5 days.

Coverage effective date: If approved, Medi-Cal coverage typically begins the first day of the month you applied (or retroactively up to 3 months back if you were eligible).

Card delivery: Your physical Medi-Cal card arrives within 10 days of approval.

What to Do After You Apply

Track your application: Create an account on BenefitsCal.org or DHCS website to check your status anytime.

Respond to requests: Medi-Cal may ask for additional information. Respond within the requested timeframe to avoid delays.

Report changes: Always notify Medi-Cal of income changes, address changes, or changes in family composition within 10 days.

Plan your renewal: Medi-Cal sends renewal notices 30 days before your coverage expires. Set a reminder to renew on time.

If Your Application is Denied

If denied, the notice letter explains why and your right to appeal. You have 90 days to request a "fair hearing" (administrative review). An appeals officer will review your case independently. Many denials are overturned on appeal, especially if income limits have changed or documentation was incomplete.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dual Eligibility

Q: Can I have both Medigap and Medi-Cal?

A: No. If you have Medi-Cal, you should NOT purchase a Medigap plan. Medi-Cal functions as your supplemental coverage (like Medigap would) by covering Medicare's cost-sharing. Having both creates coordination problems and is wasteful. Medi-Cal is superior because it also covers services Medigap doesn't (long-term care, dental, vision, hearing).

Q: How does Medi-Cal coordinate with prescription drugs?

A: You enroll in a Medicare Part D plan for basic drug coverage. Medi-Cal then assists with Part D copayments and deductibles. If Medi-Cal covers a drug that Part D rejects, Medi-Cal covers it separately. For best coordination, enroll in a D-SNP plan which integrates both.

Q: What if my income changes after I get Medi-Cal?

A: Report changes to Medi-Cal within 10 days. If income rises above limits, you lose Medi-Cal but likely qualify for Medicare Savings Programs instead. If income decreases, you might qualify for better benefits. Either way, you're better off reporting honestly.

Q: Do I lose Medi-Cal if I move to a different California county?

A: No, your Medi-Cal coverage transfers with you. However, tell Medi-Cal about the address change immediately. If your D-SNP plan doesn't serve your new county, you're entitled to switch plans during a Special Enrollment Period without waiting for annual open enrollment.

Q: Will getting Medi-Cal affect my Social Security benefits?

A: No. Medi-Cal is healthcare only; it doesn't touch your Social Security benefits. However, the income from Social Security is counted when determining Medi-Cal eligibility. (This is fair and expected; Medi-Cal expects to consider all income sources.)

Q: Can I choose my own doctors with Medi-Cal?

A: With Original Medicare + Medi-Cal: mostly yes, but check first. With a D-SNP plan: you must use network doctors, but networks are typically broad and include most providers. Before enrolling in a D-SNP, verify your preferred doctors are in-network.

Q: How much does it cost to apply for Medi-Cal?

A: Nothing. Medi-Cal application is completely free. Be suspicious of anyone claiming they charge to help you apply. Free help is available through county offices and nonprofit organizations.

Q: What if I'm approved? When does coverage start?

A: Coverage typically begins the first day of the month you applied. In some cases, coverage can be retroactive up to 3 months before you applied (if you met eligibility criteria during those months). You receive your Medi-Cal card within 10 days of approval.

Q: How long does Medi-Cal last? Do I need to renew?

A: Medi-Cal coverage is annual. You receive a renewal notice 30 days before expiration. You can renew online, by phone, or in person. If you don't renew, coverage lapses. Set a calendar reminder to avoid gaps in coverage.

Q: What if I'm denied? Can I appeal?

A: Yes. Your denial notice explains the reason and your appeal rights. You have 90 days to request a "fair hearing." An independent hearing officer reviews your case. Many appeals succeed, especially if circumstances have changed or documentation was incomplete the first time.

Q: Can I get help with my application if I don't understand English?

A: Yes. Medi-Cal provides free interpretation in multiple languages. When you call or visit an office, request an interpreter. The BenefitsCal website has language options. Free community organizations also provide application help in many languages.
