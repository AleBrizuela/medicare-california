What is Medicare Advantage

Medicare Advantage, officially called Part C, is an alternative way to receive your Medicare benefits. Instead of using Original Medicare (Parts A and B) plus separate prescription drug coverage (Part D), Medicare Advantage is a single integrated plan offered by private insurance companies that bundles hospital coverage, medical coverage, and prescription drug coverage into one plan.

In 2026, Medicare Advantage is one of the fastest-growing Medicare options nationwide, with over 28 million beneficiaries enrolled. California leads the nation with some of the highest Medicare Advantage enrollment rates and the most innovative plan designs. Over 60% of California Medicare beneficiaries choose Medicare Advantage over Original Medicare.

Key Advantage: Medicare Advantage typically costs $0-40 per month in California, and many plans include extra benefits like dental, vision, hearing, fitness programs, and transportation that Original Medicare doesn't cover at all.

How Medicare Advantage Works in California

Understanding how Medicare Advantage functions helps you evaluate whether it fits your needs and how to compare plans effectively.

The Coverage Model

When you enroll in Medicare Advantage, Medicare pays the insurance company a fixed monthly amount to cover your care. In return, the insurance company assumes the financial risk and is responsible for providing all your covered services. This model allows insurers to offer lower premiums and extra benefits because they manage the overall cost of your care.

Network-Based Coverage

Medicare Advantage plans operate using provider networks. This is the biggest difference from Original Medicare. You must use in-network doctors, specialists, and hospitals for coverage (except emergencies). Out-of-network care is typically not covered unless it's an emergency situation. Some plans offer broader networks than others, so this is a critical factor when comparing options.

Coordinated Care Model

Medicare Advantage plans emphasize coordinated care. You select a primary care doctor who manages your overall care and must provide referrals for specialist visits. This approach can improve care coordination and help prevent unnecessary duplicate tests or procedures. However, it requires more active management on your part compared to Original Medicare's open-access model.

Out-of-Pocket Maximum

Medicare Advantage plans include an out-of-pocket maximum (typically $6,700-$7,550 for 2026) that caps your annual costs. Once you reach this limit, the plan covers 100% of remaining covered services. Original Medicare has no such cap, making out-of-pocket costs potentially unlimited. This protection is valuable if you face serious illness or injury.

Prior Authorization Requirements

Many Medicare Advantage plans require prior authorization before certain services, meaning the plan must approve non-emergency procedures before you receive them. This doesn't mean you can't get the care, but it requires your doctor to request approval first. Original Medicare has no such requirements.

Network Reality: Before enrolling in any Medicare Advantage plan, verify that your primary care doctor, specialists you see regularly, and your preferred hospital are in-network. Network changes are a frequent complaint among Medicare Advantage members.

Top Medicare Advantage Carriers in California

California has exceptional Medicare Advantage options with several major national carriers plus innovative California-based companies. Here's an overview of the top seven Medicare Advantage carriers in the state.

1. Kaiser Permanente

Kaiser Permanente is California's dominant Medicare Advantage provider, covering approximately 40% of all MA members in the state. Kaiser operates an integrated health system with owned hospitals, medical offices, and pharmacy networks, allowing seamless coordinated care. Their Medicare Advantage plans typically cost $0-15 per month with strong preventive benefits, low copays ($10-20 for doctor visits), and high member satisfaction ratings. Kaiser's main limitation is that you must use their facilities and doctors, though this integrated approach often results in better care coordination. Kaiser is particularly strong in the San Francisco Bay Area and Southern California.

2. SCAN Health Plan

SCAN Health Plan specializes exclusively in Medicare and is a pioneer in senior-focused care. Available throughout California, SCAN offers comprehensive benefits including $0 dental, $0 vision, hearing aid discounts, fitness programs, and meals delivered to members with certain conditions. SCAN is particularly popular among dual-eligible beneficiaries (those with both Medicare and Medi-Cal). Plans typically range from $0-30 monthly premiums with moderate copays. SCAN's dedicated focus on seniors' unique needs has earned them consistently high quality ratings and member satisfaction scores.

3. UnitedHealthcare

UnitedHealthcare is one of the nation's largest Medicare Advantage carriers with extensive presence in California. They offer multiple plan options across the state with various network models including HMO and PPO plans. UnitedHealthcare plans typically cost $0-40 per month with benefits ranging from basic to comprehensive. Their main advantage is flexibility, offering multiple options to fit different preferences. However, customer service ratings vary, so individual plan reviews are important when evaluating their options.

4. Humana

Humana offers competitive Medicare Advantage plans throughout California with $0-35 monthly premiums. Humana plans often include supplemental benefits like dental, vision, and fitness programs. They emphasize digital tools and telehealth options, making healthcare access convenient for tech-savvy seniors. Humana's product diversity means options exist for different needs and budgets, though comparing their specific plans is essential since benefits vary widely.

5. Aetna Medicare

Aetna Medicare (CVS Aetna) offers innovative Medicare Advantage plans in California with $0-30 monthly premiums. Aetna emphasizes integrated healthcare with partnerships to prescription fill convenience. Many Aetna plans include dental, vision, hearing, and wellness benefits. As part of CVS Health, they're increasingly integrating pharmacy benefits with medical care for comprehensive coverage. Their plans are available in major California markets with good provider network breadth.

6. Alignment Healthcare

Alignment Healthcare is a newer, California-focused Medicare Advantage company emphasizing personalized care and lower copays compared to competitors. They offer plans with significant dental, vision, hearing, and fitness benefits. Alignment's plans typically feature $0-25 monthly premiums with $10-30 doctor visit copays. As a growing California company, they're building strong local relationships and reputation. However, their presence is more limited than national carriers in some areas.

7. Blue Shield of California

Blue Shield of California offers Medicare Advantage plans with extensive provider networks, high member satisfaction ratings, and strong customer service. Their plans typically cost $0-40 per month with comprehensive coverage and moderate copays. Blue Shield emphasizes preventive care and health management, reflecting the California health system's progressive approach. They serve both HMO and PPO plan options, providing flexibility in network choices.

Plan Comparison by County

Medicare Advantage availability varies significantly by county in California, with urban and suburban areas typically having 20-30 plan options while rural counties may have 5-10. The carriers listed above serve most California counties, but specific plans available in your county depend on your ZIP code and location.

Top Medicare Advantage Markets in California

Los Angeles County: Over 25 Medicare Advantage plans available, with Kaiser Permanente, Blue Shield, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, SCAN, and Alignment Healthcare all offering multiple options. Excellent competition drives innovation and beneficiary choice.

San Francisco Bay Area: Kaiser Permanente dominates with over 40% market share, but SCAN, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, and Blue Shield offer competitive alternatives. The Bay Area's large senior population supports substantial plan diversity.

San Diego County: Vibrant Medicare Advantage market with Kaiser, SCAN, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, Aetna, and Alignment Healthcare competing. Strong plan options across all benefit levels.

Inland Empire (Riverside/San Bernardino): Growing Medicare Advantage market with 15-20 plans available. Kaiser, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and SCAN are primary options. Increasing local competition is improving benefits and premiums.

Sacramento Region: Moderate plan options with Kaiser Permanente, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, SCAN, and Blue Shield available. Good coverage for California's capital region seniors.

How to Check Your County's Available Plans

Visit Medicare.gov and use their plan finder tool. Enter your ZIP code, and the system will display all Medicare Advantage plans available in your specific county along with detailed benefits, costs, and provider networks. This is the most accurate way to see your actual options since availability is highly location-specific.

Finding $0 Premium Medicare Advantage Plans

One of California's greatest Medicare Advantage advantages is the prevalence of $0 premium plans. In 2026, over 70% of California Medicare Advantage plans have zero monthly premiums, compared to national averages around 50%. This reflects California's large senior population, competitive insurance markets, and robust Medicare Advantage enrollment.

Why Are So Many Plans $0 Premium?

Insurance companies can offer $0 premiums because Medicare pays them a fixed monthly amount per member based on demographic risk factors. Younger, healthier Medicare beneficiaries cost Medicare less, allowing insurers to offer low premiums. Additionally, plans with $0 premiums often have higher copays ($30-50 per visit) and stricter networks, offsetting their low premium costs. The insurers profit by controlling utilization through these higher cost-sharing mechanisms.

Don't Choose Based on Premium Alone

This is critical: two $0 premium plans can have vastly different total costs. One plan might have $20 doctor visit copays while another has $50 copays. If you visit your doctor monthly, the difference is $360 annually. Using Medicare.gov's cost estimator tool, you can calculate your estimated total costs including premiums, copays, and deductibles based on your anticipated healthcare needs and specific medications. This is the only way to truly compare plans.

Highest-Value $0 Premium Plans in California

Kaiser Permanente's $0 plans offer particularly good value with low copays ($10-15) and minimal prior authorization requirements. SCAN's $0 plans for dual-eligible members provide exceptional value with extensive supplemental benefits. Several regional carriers now offer $0-5 plans with reasonable copay structures ($25-35) and decent provider networks. Always run the cost estimator regardless of premium.

Special Needs Plans and D-SNPs

Special Needs Plans (SNPs) are specialized Medicare Advantage plans designed for people with specific chronic conditions or circumstances. California offers several SNP types, with D-SNPs being particularly valuable for dual-eligible beneficiaries.

Chronic Condition Special Needs Plans (C-SNPs)

C-SNPs target people with specific chronic diseases such as diabetes, congestive heart failure, COPD, asthma, cancer, stroke, and kidney disease. These plans provide specialized care management, disease-specific programs, medication therapy management, and provider networks focused on condition specialists. If you have one of these conditions, C-SNPs can provide superior care coordination compared to general Medicare Advantage plans. Enrollment is restricted to people with the specific qualifying condition.

Dual-Eligible Special Needs Plans (D-SNPs)

D-SNPs are exclusively for people eligible for both Medicare and Medi-Cal, typically low-income seniors. D-SNPs in California provide exceptional benefits that far exceed standard Medicare Advantage. Many D-SNPs include zero-cost dental (full coverage), zero-cost vision, zero-cost hearing aids, meal delivery programs, transportation services, and other wraparound benefits. D-SNPs are particularly valuable because Medi-Cal covers Medicare premiums and cost-sharing, often resulting in truly comprehensive coverage with minimal out-of-pocket costs.

For dual-eligible Californians with limited income, D-SNPs represent the best healthcare value available, often eliminating out-of-pocket costs entirely while providing benefits far superior to standard Medicare Advantage or Original Medicare. Carriers like SCAN, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and others operate popular D-SNPs throughout California.

Institutional Special Needs Plans (I-SNPs)

I-SNPs serve people in institutional settings (nursing homes, assisted living). If you're considering or currently in a facility, I-SNPs provide specialized coverage for your situation. Availability is more limited than C-SNPs and D-SNPs, but important options exist for institutional settings.

D-SNP Value: If you qualify for Medi-Cal, strongly consider D-SNPs. The combination of Medicare, Medi-Cal, and specialized benefits often provides better coverage than any other Medicare option available.

How to Compare Medicare Advantage Plans

Effective plan comparison requires looking beyond premiums to understand total costs, networks, benefits, and plan quality. Here's a structured approach.

Step 1: Identify Your Healthcare Priorities

List your anticipated healthcare needs: How often do you see your primary care doctor? Which specialists do you see regularly? How many prescription medications do you take? Do you need dental or vision care? Have any specific health conditions? This assessment drives your plan selection. Different plan types suit different healthcare patterns.

Step 2: Verify Provider Networks

List your primary care doctor, the specialists you see regularly, and your preferred hospital. For each Medicare Advantage plan you're considering, verify that these providers are in-network and accepting new Medicare Advantage patients. Network changes are a major point of frustration for plan members, so understanding whether your doctors are in-network is essential. Many plans allow you to search provider networks on their websites.

Step 3: Review Benefit Coverage

Check specific coverage for services you use or anticipate needing: prescription drugs (check formulas for your specific medications), mental health services (therapy and psychiatric care), vision and dental (coverage limits and copays), hearing (hearing aids or exams), and any other specialized services. Plans vary dramatically in supplemental benefits, so detailed review matters.

Step 4: Use Medicare's Cost Estimator Tool

Visit Medicare.gov and use their official plan comparison and cost estimator tools. Enter your specific medications and anticipated doctor visits, and the system calculates your estimated total 2026 costs for each plan including premiums, copays, and deductibles. This is far more accurate than comparing plans based on premium alone. Don't skip this step.

Step 5: Check Plan Quality Ratings

Medicare publishes Star Ratings for all plans based on member satisfaction, quality measures, and customer service. Plans range from 2.5 stars to 5 stars. Higher-rated plans typically provide better care coordination, customer service, and member outcomes. However, don't automatically choose the highest-rated plan if it doesn't include your doctors or meet your specific healthcare needs.

Step 6: Calculate Out-of-Pocket Risk

Medicare Advantage plans include annual out-of-pocket maximums ($6,700-$7,550 for 2026), meaning costs are capped. However, be aware of copay structures. Some plans front-load costs with high copays while others use lower copays but higher deductibles. Consider your worst-case scenario (major illness/hospitalization) and best-case scenario (minimal healthcare needs) and evaluate how different plans handle both.

Enrollment Periods for Medicare Advantage

Understanding Medicare Advantage enrollment deadlines is critical because missing enrollment windows may lock you out of plan changes until the next enrollment period.

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP): October 15 - December 7

This is the main enrollment period for Medicare Advantage changes. If you're already in a Medicare Advantage plan and want to switch to a different MA plan or return to Original Medicare, you can do so during AEP. If you're in Original Medicare and want to enroll in Medicare Advantage, you can do this during AEP. Changes take effect January 1. You can enroll in one new plan during this period.

Open Enrollment Period (OEP): January 1 - March 31

During OEP, Original Medicare beneficiaries can switch to Medicare Advantage, and Medicare Advantage beneficiaries can return to Original Medicare. However, you cannot use OEP to switch from one MA plan to another MA plan. If you're already in a Medicare Advantage plan and want to try a different one, you must wait for AEP (October-December). OEP was created specifically to allow people in Original Medicare to move to Medicare Advantage after they see their actual healthcare expenses.

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

When you first become eligible for Medicare at age 65, you have a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period: 3 months before your birthday month, your birthday month itself, and 3 months after. During IEP, you can enroll in Medicare Advantage without waiting for AEP.

Special Enrollment Periods (SEP)

If you experience qualifying life events, you may be eligible for a Special Enrollment Period allowing you to change Medicare Advantage plans outside the normal AEP window. Qualifying events include moving out of your plan's service area, losing employer coverage, getting married or divorced, gaining Medi-Cal eligibility, or certain other circumstances. If a qualifying event occurs, contact Medicare immediately to determine your SEP eligibility and deadlines.

Effective Dates for 2026 Medicare Advantage Changes

Plans chosen during October-December 2025 become effective January 1, 2026. Plans chosen during January-March 2026 OEP become effective the first of the following month (not immediate). Plan choices made during other Special Enrollment Periods have specific effective dates depending on the SEP type. Always confirm the effective date before enrolling.

Medicare Advantage vs Original Medicare: Pros and Cons

Choosing between Medicare Advantage and Original Medicare is a major decision. Understanding the trade-offs helps you select the approach that fits your healthcare needs, provider preferences, and financial situation.

Medicare Advantage Pros

  • Lower Premiums: Most California Medicare Advantage plans cost $0-40/month versus $185+ for Part B alone plus separate Part D and Medigap costs.
  • All-in-One Coverage: Parts A, B, and D are bundled into one plan with one card and one plan year, simplifying administration.
  • Extra Benefits: Dental, vision, hearing, fitness, and meal programs are often included at no or low additional cost.
  • Out-of-Pocket Cap: Annual out-of-pocket maximum limits your risk during serious illness or injury.
  • Care Coordination: Primary care doctors coordinate your overall care, potentially reducing duplicate tests and improving care quality.
  • Prescription Drugs Integrated: No separate Part D enrollment required; medications are covered as part of the plan.

Medicare Advantage Cons

  • Network Restrictions: You must use in-network doctors and hospitals (except emergencies). Out-of-network care is typically not covered or very expensive.
  • Higher Copays: Doctor visit copays of $20-50 and specialist copays of $30-80 add up if you see providers frequently.
  • Prior Authorization: Many services require plan approval before delivery, which can delay care and add administrative burden.
  • Referral Requirements: Seeing specialists typically requires primary care doctor referrals, limiting access to self-referred specialists.
  • Plan Changes: Insurance companies change provider networks and benefits annually, meaning your doctor or pharmacy might be dropped.
  • Care Limitations: Plans may restrict care or impose utilization limits that Original Medicare doesn't.
  • Travel Challenges: Coverage outside your plan's service area is limited to emergencies, complicating extended travel or snowbird living.

Original Medicare Pros

  • Provider Choice: See any doctor accepting Medicare anywhere in the U.S. without referrals.
  • No Prior Authorization: Doctors can order tests and treatments without insurance approval.
  • Stability: Original Medicare coverage doesn't change year-to-year, providing predictability.
  • Travel Friendly: Original Medicare works nationwide and internationally in many cases.
  • Specialist Access: See specialists directly without referrals or coordination through a primary care doctor.

Original Medicare Cons

  • Higher Premiums: Part B costs $185/month standard, Part D (separate) averages $30-50/month, and Medigap ranges from $100-350+/month.
  • No Out-of-Pocket Cap: Your costs are theoretically unlimited if you face serious illness.
  • Cost Complexity: Three separate enrollments (Part B, Part D, Medigap) with different deadlines and annual changes.
  • No Extra Benefits: Dental, vision, hearing, and other services aren't covered unless you buy separate policies.
  • Coverage Gaps: Medigap plans only partially cover costs, and no Medigap plan covers 100% of all services.

How to Choose the Right Medicare Advantage Plan

Making this decision requires honestly assessing your healthcare situation and priorities.

Choose Medicare Advantage If:

  • You want comprehensive coverage with low or zero monthly premiums and all-in-one simplicity.
  • Your doctors and preferred hospital are in-network with the plan you're considering.
  • You have stable healthcare needs and see the same doctors regularly (predictable networks).
  • You want extra benefits like dental, vision, fitness, or meal programs included.
  • You're comfortable with copays at doctor visits in exchange for lower premiums.
  • You like care coordination through a primary care doctor managing your overall care.
  • You want a guaranteed annual out-of-pocket maximum that limits your financial risk.
  • You qualify for Medi-Cal and can enroll in a D-SNP with exceptional benefits.

Choose Original Medicare + Medigap If:

  • Provider choice and doctor flexibility are your top priorities.
  • You see many different specialists who may not all be in-network with MA plans.
  • You want the ability to see any doctor anywhere without referrals or approvals.
  • You travel frequently or spend time in multiple states (snowbirds).
  • You value care continuity and want to avoid provider network changes.
  • You're willing to pay higher premiums for maximum flexibility.
  • You have complex medical needs requiring frequent specialist consultation.
  • You want predictable costs year-over-year without sudden benefit changes.

Decision Framework

Start by identifying your most important value: Is it affordability? Provider choice? Simplicity? Comprehensive coverage? Once you identify your primary value, let that guide your plan type choice. Then, within your chosen type, use Medicare.gov's cost estimator and plan comparison tools to identify the specific plan that best meets your needs.

Most importantly, verify that your doctors are actually in-network if you choose Medicare Advantage. Provider access is the #1 reason MA members switch plans, so confirming network adequacy before enrolling is absolutely critical.
