From established neighborhoods in downtown Riverside to newer retirement communities in Murrieta, Temecula, and Hemet, the region offers diverse healthcare options. ZIP codes like 92501, 92553, and 92880 serve different neighborhoods with varying distances to major medical centers, which influences plan choice and healthcare access. Frequently Asked Questions

How many Medicare plans are available in Riverside County?

Riverside County typically has 45+ Medicare Advantage plans available, depending on your specific ZIP code. Additionally, you can choose Traditional Medicare (Parts A & B) with Medigap supplemental insurance and standalone Part D prescription drug plans. The robust selection reflects the Inland Empire's growing senior population and strong plan competition. Our agents can help you evaluate all options specific to your Riverside neighborhood and healthcare preferences.

Can I keep my Loma Linda University Health or Riverside Community Hospital doctor with Medicare?

Most Medicare plans in Riverside County include Loma Linda University Health and Riverside Community Hospital in their networks, though coverage varies by specific plan. Kaiser Permanente has its own network and may not include independent hospital providers. Always verify your preferred doctor and facility are in-network before enrolling. Our agents can check provider networks for any plan you're considering, ensuring you maintain continuity of care.

When is the best time to enroll in Medicare in Riverside County?

You're typically eligible at age 65 with a 7-month Initial Enrollment Period to enroll without penalties. Many Riverside residents use the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15-December 7) to review and switch plans. If you're retiring or losing employer coverage, you may qualify for a Special Enrollment Period. Our agents can help explain your options and timeline, making sure you understand the best enrollment strategy for your situation.
